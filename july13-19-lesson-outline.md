# July 13-19 Lesson Plan Outline

## He Trusted in the Lord God of Israel

**Scriptures:** 2 Kings 16-25

**Main message:** When faith is challenged, the Lord invites us to trust Him, return to His word, and keep our covenants with all our heart and soul.

## Opening

Ask:

**What helps you remember to trust Heavenly Father and Jesus Christ when life feels stressful or confusing?**

Explain:

These chapters describe difficult years for Israel and Judah. The Northern Kingdom was conquered and scattered. Judah also struggled with idolatry and spiritual decline. But Hezekiah and Josiah stand out as examples of turning to the Lord. Hezekiah trusted the Lord when Jerusalem was threatened. Josiah let the scriptures turn his heart and renew his covenant.

## 1. I Can Stay True When My Faith Is Challenged

**Brief introduction:** The Assyrian army threatened Jerusalem and tried to weaken the people's faith. Their message was not just military intimidation. It was a direct attempt to make Judah doubt the Lord.

**Read:** 2 Kings 18:5-7, 28-30; 2 Kings 19:1, 14-19

**Summarize:** 2 Kings 18:31-35; 2 Kings 19:10-13

**Teaching points:**

- Hezekiah "trusted in the Lord God of Israel."
- He "clave to the Lord" and kept His commandments.
- The Assyrians tried to make the people doubt before the crisis was resolved.
- Their taunts promised an easier life, compared the Lord to powerless idols, and accused trust in God of being foolish.
- Hezekiah took the threat into the house of the Lord.
- Faith includes bringing fear honestly to God.

**Discussion questions:**

- What do you notice about Hezekiah in 2 Kings 18:5-7?
- What reasons did the Assyrians give for not trusting the Lord?
- What similar messages challenge faith today?

**Quiet reflection: Spread it before the Lord**

Before reading 2 Kings 19:14-19, invite the class to quietly think of a worry, decision, or pressure they could take to the Lord. They do not need to share it. As the verses are read, ask them to notice what Hezekiah did with his fear and what he remembered about God. Testify that we can bring real concerns to Him.

**After reading 2 Kings 19:14-19, ask:**

- What did Hezekiah do with the message that worried him?
- What did Hezekiah remember about God in his prayer?
- Why do you think it helps to bring specific worries to the Lord?
- What can we learn from the way Hezekiah prayed?

**Simple summary:** Hezekiah teaches us to take fear to the Lord instead of letting fear lead us away from Him.

## 2. All Things Are in the Lord's Hands

**Brief introduction:** Sennacherib had strong armies and a record of conquest. From a worldly view, Jerusalem looked powerless. But the Lord answered Hezekiah through the prophet Isaiah and showed that earthly power is not greater than God's purposes.

**Read:** 2 Kings 19:20, 32, 34

**Summarize:** 2 Kings 19:21-31, 33, 35-37

**Teaching points:**

- The Lord heard Hezekiah's prayer.
- Isaiah gave the Lord's answer before deliverance came.
- Sennacherib misunderstood his own power.
- The Lord protected Jerusalem in a way Judah could not have produced alone.
- Trusting the Lord does not mean we see the whole picture right away.

**Discussion questions:**

- What truths about the Lord do you see in His answer through Isaiah?
- Why might it matter that the Lord said, "I have heard," before Jerusalem was delivered?
- How can remembering that all things are in the Lord's hands help us during uncertainty?

**Simple summary:** The Lord sees more than we see and can act in ways we cannot predict.

## 3. The Scriptures Can Turn My Heart to the Lord

**Brief introduction:** Before Josiah became king, Judah had been led into serious idolatry under Manasseh. The people needed more than a small adjustment; they needed to turn back to the Lord. During Josiah's reign, the book of the law was found in the house of the Lord. When Josiah heard the words of scripture, he recognized that the people needed to change. The scriptures did not just inform him. They moved him to repentance and reform.

**Read:** 2 Kings 21:2-3; 2 Kings 22:8, 11; 2 Kings 23:2-3, 25

**Summarize:** 2 Kings 21:1-6; 2 Kings 22:9-13; 2 Kings 23:1-6, 21, 24

**Teaching points:**

- Manasseh's reign shows how far Judah had turned from the Lord.
- Josiah listened to scripture with a humble heart.
- The word of God helped him see what needed to change.
- Josiah did not stop with feeling sorry. He acted.
- Scripture can reveal truth, invite repentance, and point us back to Jesus Christ.
- The scriptures can "work mightily" in us when we receive them with faith.

**Discussion questions:**

- Why do you think Josiah reacted so strongly when he heard the book of the law?
- What is the difference between reading scripture and letting scripture change us?
- When has a scripture helped you see something differently?
- What helps scripture study feel like a conversation with the Lord instead of just a task?

**Object lesson: The hidden scriptures**

Before class, hide a set of scriptures or a printed verse somewhere in the room. Invite the class to find it. After it is found, ask what might happen if God's word was present but ignored or forgotten. Connect this to Josiah finding the book of the law in the temple.

**Simple summary:** Scripture can turn our hearts when we let it show us both God's mercy and what needs to change.

## 4. A Covenant Is a Whole-Souled Commitment

**Brief introduction:** Josiah gathered the people, read the words of the covenant, and made a covenant before the Lord. The people stood to the covenant, and Josiah worked to remove idolatry from the land.

**Read:** 2 Kings 23:2-3, 25

**Summarize:** 2 Kings 23:21-24

**Teaching points:**

- Josiah's covenant involved heart, soul, and action.
- The people heard the word of God together.
- Covenant commitment led to visible changes.
- Keeping covenants is more than avoiding wrong. It is choosing to walk after the Lord.
- Jesus Christ gives power to repent, return, and remain faithful.

**Discussion questions:**

- What words in 2 Kings 23:3 show the depth of Josiah's commitment?
- What does it mean to "walk after the Lord"?
- How do covenants help us know what to remove, repair, or strengthen?
- How have your covenants helped you during a difficult time?

**Simple activity: Whole heart, whole soul**

Write these phrases on the board: **heart**, **soul**, **might**, **walk after the Lord**. Ask the class to list what each phrase could look like during a normal week. Invite everyone to choose one quiet way to keep a covenant more intentionally this week.

**Simple summary:** Covenants are whole-souled commitments that help us walk after Jesus Christ.

## 5. The Book of Mormon Begins in This Same Crisis

**Brief introduction:** The Old Testament and the Book of Mormon overlap at the end of Judah's kingdom. Zedekiah was the last king of Judah before Jerusalem was destroyed. The Book of Mormon begins in Jerusalem during the first year of Zedekiah's reign, when Lehi and many other prophets warned the people to repent.

**Read:** 2 Kings 24:18-19; 1 Nephi 1:4

**Summarize:** 2 Kings 25:1-10; 1 Nephi 1:13, 18-20

**Teaching points:**

- Lehi lived in Jerusalem during the same historical moment described at the end of 2 Kings.
- Zedekiah's reign helps us place the beginning of the Book of Mormon in Old Testament history.
- Many prophets warned Jerusalem to repent before the destruction came.
- Lehi's family left Jerusalem before the destruction because Lehi listened to revelation.
- The Lord keeps warning, guiding, and delivering those who are willing to hear Him.

**Discussion questions:**

- Why does it matter that the Book of Mormon begins during a real Old Testament crisis?
- What does 1 Nephi 1:4 teach us about the Lord's mercy before judgment comes?
- How was Lehi's response different from many people in Jerusalem?
- What warnings or guidance does the Lord give us today through prophets?

**Simple summary:** The Book of Mormon begins as Judah is nearing destruction, showing that the Lord was still speaking, warning, and preparing a way for those who would listen.

## Closing Discussion

Ask:

**What is one reform the Lord may be inviting you to make this week?**

Possible answers:

- Spread one worry before the Lord in prayer.
- Trust the Lord when faith is challenged.
- Study a scripture until it works in you.
- Remove one spiritual distraction.
- Keep one covenant more intentionally.

## Closing Testimony

Hezekiah trusted the Lord when fear and enemy voices were loud. Josiah let the scriptures turn his heart and then acted on what he learned. Their examples show that reform is possible even in spiritually difficult times. Jesus Christ hears our prayers, speaks through scripture, honors covenants, and helps His people return. We can trust Him with all our heart and soul.

## Invitation

Choose one action this week:

- Take one worry to the Lord in prayer.
- Read one scripture slowly and ask, "What would the Lord have me change?"
- Remove one distraction that weakens your faith.
- Keep one covenant with more intention.
