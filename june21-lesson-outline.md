# June 21 Lesson Plan Outline

## The Battle Is the Lord's

**Scriptures:** 1 Samuel 17-18; 24-26; 2 Samuel 5-7

**Main message:** With faith in Jesus Christ, we can face great challenges, forgive others, receive direction, and trust the Lord to fight our spiritual battles.

## Opening

Ask:

**What makes a challenge feel like a giant?**

Explain that David's confidence did not come from his size, weapons, or experience as a soldier. It came from remembering how the Lord had helped him before and trusting that the Lord would help him again.

## 1. With the Lord's Help, I Can Face Great Challenges

**Brief introduction:** Israel's army was afraid of Goliath. David was young and had no military armor, but he knew that the Lord had delivered him from danger before. He trusted God's power more than Goliath's strength.

**Read:** 1 Samuel 17:11, 26, 32, 34-37, 39-40, 45-47

**Video:** [The Lord Will Deliver Me](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/media/video/2011-03-0066-the-lord-will-deliver-me?lang=eng)

**Teaching points:**

- David remembered what the Lord had already done for him.
- He did not depend on Saul's armor or try to become someone else.
- David testified of the Lord before the battle was won.
- Faith does not mean a challenge is small. It means God is greater than the challenge.

**Discussion questions:**

- Why was David confident when everyone else was afraid?
- What past experiences can remind us that the Lord will help us?
- What are some modern Goliaths that youth or families face?
- What does it mean that "the battle is the Lord's"?

**Conference talk:** [Invite Christ to Author Your Story](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/10/42johnson?lang=eng), President Camille N. Johnson

**Simple summary:** David faced Goliath with the Lord instead of facing him alone.

## 2. One Person with Faith in Christ Can Make a Difference

**Brief introduction:** David's individual faith changed the direction of an entire battle. His example shows that one faithful person can bring courage and hope to many others.

**Read:** 1 Samuel 17:26-50

**Conference talk:** [Faithful to the End](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2024/04/33spannaus?lang=eng), Sister Andrea Munoz Spannaus

### Five Stones Activity

Give everyone five paper stones, or draw five stones on a board. Sister Spannaus suggested five strengths:

- Love for God
- Faith in Jesus Christ
- Knowledge of our true identity
- Daily repentance
- Access to God's power through covenants

Ask:

- Which stone do you feel is strongest in your life?
- Which one would you like to strengthen?
- What additional spiritual stone would you add?

**Simple summary:** Small acts of faith can have an influence much larger than we expect.

## 3. Good Friends Can Be a Blessing from God

**Context:** These events happen immediately after David defeats Goliath and speaks with King Saul. Jonathan was Saul's son and a likely heir to the throne. Instead of treating David as a threat, Jonathan formed a covenant friendship with him and loved him "as his own soul." Saul kept David in royal service rather than allowing him to return permanently to his father's house.

When David returned from defeating Goliath, women from Israel's cities celebrated the victory and sang that Saul had slain thousands and David ten thousands. Their song was not necessarily intended to reject Saul, but Saul received it through comparison and insecurity. He became angry and began to view David as a rival who might take the kingdom.

The passage deliberately contrasts two reactions to David's success: Jonathan responded with covenant love, loyalty, and generosity, while Saul responded with jealousy, fear, and suspicion. Jonathan had more personally to lose if David became king, yet he chose friendship instead of rivalry. This context helps show how comparison can distort relationships and how Christlike love can overcome competition.

**Read:** 1 Samuel 18:1-4, 6-9

**Teaching points:**

- Saul compared himself with David and became jealous.
- Jonathan celebrated David instead of competing with him.
- A Christlike friend helps us become faithful to God.
- Jonathan's loyalty and sacrifice can remind us of Jesus Christ.

**Discussion questions:**

- What was different about Saul's and Jonathan's reactions to David?
- How can comparison damage friendship?
- What qualities make someone a Christlike friend?
- How can we support a friend when something good happens to them?

**Simple summary:** Christlike friendship celebrates, protects, and strengthens others.

## 4. Forgiveness Is a Path to Peace and Healing

**Brief introduction:** Saul repeatedly tried to kill David. David had opportunities to harm Saul but refused to take revenge. Abigail also responded calmly and wisely when anger could have led to tragedy.

**Read:** 1 Samuel 24:4-7, 16-20; 1 Samuel 25:23-35; 1 Samuel 26:9-11

**Teaching points:**

- David practiced restraint when revenge seemed possible.
- Abigail used humility, courage, and wisdom to make peace.
- Forgiveness does not mean approving harmful behavior.
- Jesus Christ can heal our hearts and help us respond with self-control.

**Discussion questions:**

- What did David choose instead of revenge?
- How did Abigail prevent anger from causing greater harm?
- What is the difference between forgiveness and allowing mistreatment?
- How can Christ help us forgive when it feels impossible?

**Conference talks:**

- [Christlike Poise](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2023/04/31bragg?lang=eng), Elder Mark A. Bragg
- [Beauty for Ashes: The Healing Path of Forgiveness](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2022/10/23yee?lang=eng), Sister Kristin M. Yee

**Excerpt from Elder Bragg's talk:**

> Let's consider elements of Christlike poise. To begin, knowing who we are and being true to our divine identity brings calm. Christlike poise requires that we avoid comparing ourselves to others or pretending to be someone we're not. Joseph Smith taught, "If men do not comprehend the character of God, they do not comprehend themselves." It is just not possible to have divine poise without knowing that we are divine sons and daughters of a loving Heavenly Father.

**Simple summary:** Christlike strength includes knowing when not to strike back.

## 5. The Lord Can Give Me Direction

**Brief introduction:** David's victory over Goliath did not mean that every future battle would be easy. As king, David continued to ask the Lord what to do instead of assuming that an earlier answer would apply to every situation.

**Read:** 2 Samuel 5:17-25

**Teaching points:**

- David "inquired of the Lord" before acting.
- He asked again when the circumstances changed.
- The Lord may give different instructions in similar situations.
- Revelation requires both asking and acting.

**Discussion questions:**

- Why did David ask the Lord again instead of repeating his previous plan?
- What decisions should we seek the Lord's help with?
- How can we recognize and act on personal revelation?

**Simple activity:** Present two similar situations and ask why the right answer might be different for different people or at different times.

**Simple summary:** Past revelation teaches us, but we still need present revelation.

## 6. Jesus Christ Is the Eternal King

**Brief introduction:** David wanted to build a house, or temple, for the Lord. The Lord promised instead to establish David's "house," meaning his posterity and kingdom. This promise was ultimately fulfilled through Jesus Christ, a descendant of David whose kingdom will never end.

**Read:** 2 Samuel 7:12-16; Luke 1:32-33; Revelation 19:16

**Teaching points:**

- Every earthly king and kingdom is temporary.
- Jesus Christ is the perfect and Eternal King.
- Christ wins battles through truth, righteousness, mercy, and redeeming power.
- We honor our King by trusting Him and keeping our covenants.

**Discussion questions:**

- How is Jesus Christ different from earthly leaders?
- What kind of kingdom does Christ establish?
- How do we show that Jesus Christ is our King?

**Simple summary:** David's greatest descendant is Jesus Christ, the King of Kings.

## Closing Discussion

Ask:

**What is one Goliath you can face differently because Jesus Christ is with you?**

## Closing Testimony

David did not overcome Goliath because he was stronger than the giant. He prevailed because he trusted the living God. The same Lord can help us face our challenges, strengthen our friendships, forgive others, receive direction, and remain faithful. Jesus Christ is our Deliverer and Eternal King. The battle is His, and we do not have to face it alone.

## Invitation

Choose one action this week:

- Remember a time when the Lord helped you.
- Strengthen one of your five spiritual stones.
- Encourage a friend instead of comparing yourself.
- Take one step toward forgiveness and healing.
- Ask the Lord for direction and act on the answer.
