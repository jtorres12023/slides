# June 29-July 5 Lesson Plan Outline

## If the Lord Be God, Follow Him

**Scriptures:** 1 Kings 12-13; 17-22

**Main message:** The Lord invites us to choose Him with undivided hearts. He can provide, correct, answer, comfort, and turn our hearts back again.

## Opening

Ask:

**What makes it difficult to follow the Lord when other voices are pulling at us?**

Explain:

These chapters show people making choices about who they will trust. Jeroboam changes worship because he fears losing influence. Elijah trusts the Lord in drought and danger. The widow of Zarephath acts in faith when she has almost nothing left. Israel is invited to stop halting between two opinions. Elijah later learns that the Lord's voice is often quiet.

## 1. Small Choices Can Turn a Heart

**Brief introduction:** After the kingdom divided, Jeroboam worried that if the northern tribes continued worshipping at Jerusalem, their hearts would return to the house of David. Instead of trusting the Lord, he created alternate worship centers and golden calves.

**Read:** 1 Kings 12:26-33

**Teaching points:**

- Jeroboam's false worship began with fear.
- He made obedience seem inconvenient: "It is too much for you to go up to Jerusalem."
- He mixed religious language with disobedience.
- False substitutes for God can feel practical, popular, or easier at first.
- The Lord asks for worship that turns our whole hearts to Him.

**Discussion questions:**

- What fear seems to be driving Jeroboam?
- Why is it spiritually dangerous to change God's commandments to fit our comfort?
- What are modern examples of "golden calves" that can compete with the Lord?
- How can small choices either turn our hearts toward God or away from Him?

**Simple summary:** Fear and convenience can quietly pull our hearts away from the Lord.

## 2. Faith Often Begins with Trust

**Brief introduction:** Elijah declared that there would be no rain except according to the Lord's word. Then the Lord sent Elijah to the brook Cherith and provided food in an unexpected way.

**Read:** 1 Kings 17:1-7

**Teaching points:**

- Elijah stood before Ahab because he first stood before the Lord.
- The Lord gave Elijah direction one step at a time.
- The Lord's provision did not remove every difficulty, but it sustained Elijah.
- Faith sometimes means going where the Lord sends us before we know the whole plan.

**Discussion questions:**

- What do you learn from Elijah's phrase "before whom I stand"?
- Why might the Lord reveal only the next step instead of the whole path?
- When has the Lord sustained you in an unexpected way?

**Simple summary:** The Lord can sustain us as we follow His word one step at a time.

## 3. The Lord Can Make Enough

**Brief introduction:** When the brook dried up, the Lord sent Elijah to a widow in Zarephath. She had only a handful of meal and a little oil. Elijah invited her to act in faith before she saw the miracle.

**Read:** 1 Kings 17:8-16

**Teaching points:**

- The widow's circumstances were desperate.
- Elijah's invitation required trust in the Lord's promise.
- The miracle came as she acted, not before.
- The Lord can make "enough" when we put Him first.
- This story points us to Jesus Christ, who multiplies, sustains, and gives life.

**Discussion questions:**

- What would have made Elijah's invitation hard to obey?
- What does it mean to put the Lord first when resources feel limited?
- How have you seen the Lord make enough from something small?

**Simple summary:** Faith may require giving the Lord our first trust, not our leftovers.

## 4. "If the Lord Be God, Follow Him"

**Brief introduction:** On Mount Carmel, Elijah invited Israel to stop wavering between the Lord and Baal. The contest was not just about fire from heaven. It was about turning hearts back to the living God.

**Read:** 1 Kings 18:17-39

**Teaching points:**

- Elijah's question exposed divided loyalty.
- The people did not answer at first.
- Elijah prayed that the people would know the Lord and that their hearts would turn back.
- The fire showed God's power, but the deeper miracle was renewed recognition: "The Lord, he is the God."
- Following the Lord means choosing Him, not merely admiring Him.

**Discussion questions:**

- Why do you think the people "answered him not a word"?
- What does it look like today to halt between two opinions?
- Why did Elijah pray for the people's hearts to turn back?
- What is one choice that shows we really believe "the Lord, he is the God"?

**Simple activity:** Write two columns on a board: **Baal voices** and **Lord's voice**. Ask the class to name messages that pull people away from God and truths that help them follow Him.

**Art activity: 4-panel Mount Carmel comic strip**

Give each person a blank 4-panel comic strip page. Invite them to draw the story from 1 Kings 18 in four simple scenes:

- Panel 1: Elijah asks, "How long halt ye between two opinions?"
- Panel 2: Elijah repairs the altar and prays to the Lord.
- Panel 3: The fire of the Lord falls.
- Panel 4: The people say, "The Lord, he is the God."

After they draw, ask:

- Which panel feels most important to you?
- What would a modern version of "follow Him" look like in your life this week?

**Simple summary:** If the Lord is God to us, we follow Him with more than words.

## 5. The Lord Often Speaks Quietly

**Brief introduction:** After Mount Carmel, Elijah became afraid and discouraged. He fled into the wilderness and felt alone. The Lord cared for his body, listened to him, and then taught him through a still small voice.

**Read:** 1 Kings 19:1-18

**Teaching points:**

- Spiritual strength does not mean we never feel exhausted or afraid.
- The Lord cared for Elijah with rest and food before giving more instruction.
- The Lord was not in the wind, earthquake, or fire in the way Elijah expected.
- Revelation often comes quietly through the Holy Ghost.
- The Lord reminded Elijah that he was not alone and still had work to do.

**Discussion questions:**

- What does this chapter teach us about discouragement?
- Why might the Lord care for Elijah physically before giving spiritual direction?
- What can make it hard to hear the still small voice?
- How can we create more room to hear the Lord?

**Simple summary:** The Lord's quiet voice can steady us when dramatic answers are not what we need most.

## 6. Beware of Wanting What Is Not Ours

**Brief introduction:** Ahab wanted Naboth's vineyard. Naboth refused because the land was his family inheritance. Ahab responded with resentment, and Jezebel used dishonesty and violence to get what Ahab wanted.

**Read:** 1 Kings 21:1-19

**Teaching points:**

- Ahab let disappointment become entitlement.
- Jezebel treated power as permission to do wrong.
- Naboth's inheritance mattered to him and to the Lord.
- Coveting can make us blind to other people's worth and rights.
- The Lord sees injustice and calls His people to integrity.

**Discussion questions:**

- What is the difference between disappointment and entitlement?
- How can wanting something too much weaken our integrity?
- Why does the Lord care about how people with power treat others?
- How can Jesus Christ help us change our desires?

**Simple summary:** Following the Lord includes honoring other people, even when we do not get what we want.

## Closing Discussion

Ask:

**Where do you feel the Lord inviting you to stop halting between two opinions?**

Possible answers:

- Choosing worship over convenience.
- Trusting the Lord with the next step.
- Putting God first when resources feel limited.
- Listening for the still small voice.
- Choosing integrity over entitlement.

## Closing Testimony

Jeroboam shows how fear and convenience can lead hearts away from God. Elijah and the widow show that the Lord can provide as we trust Him. Mount Carmel teaches that divided loyalty cannot bring lasting peace. Horeb teaches that the Lord often speaks quietly, personally, and mercifully. Jesus Christ is the living Lord who turns hearts back again. If He is God to us, we follow Him.

## Invitation

Choose one action this week:

- Remove one false substitute for God.
- Trust the Lord with one first step.
- Put Him first in a difficult choice.
- Make quiet space to hear the Holy Ghost.
- Choose integrity when desire pulls hard.
